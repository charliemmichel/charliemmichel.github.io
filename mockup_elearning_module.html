Slide 1 of 24 Introduction
What this tool does (and why it matters to your workflow)

This AI tool analyzes customer conversations and surfaces actionable insights in real time. It integrates directly into Salesforce and Slack.

  • For Customer Success: Automatically flags at-risk accounts based on sentiment analysis and engagement patterns.
  • For Sales Leadership: Tracks competitive mentions, objection patterns, and deal velocity across your pipeline.

This module will show you how to set up alerts, interpret insights, and integrate the tool into your daily workflow.

Slide 8 of 24 Scenario
Why AI insights fail (and how to avoid it)
"We rolled out sentiment tracking to the whole CS team. Everyone got alerts. But six weeks later, nobody was actually using it. The alerts just sat there unread." CS Director, post-implementation review

AI tools do not fail because of the technology. They fail because of how they are deployed.

  • Alert fatigue is real. If every alert is urgent, no alert is urgent. You need to configure thresholds that match your workflow, not default settings.
  • Insights without action are noise. The tool shows you what is happening. You still need to decide what to do about it.
  • Adoption requires integration. If the tool lives outside your existing workflow, you will not use it. We will show you how to embed it into the systems you already work in.
Slide 15 of 24 Knowledge Check
You are a Customer Success Manager. The AI flags an account with declining sentiment and decreasing engagement. What should you do first?
A
Schedule an immediate escalation call with the account executive and CS leadership.
B
Wait to see if sentiment improves over the next two weeks before taking action.
C
Review recent call transcripts and Slack threads to understand context, then reach out directly to the primary contact.
D
Turn off sentiment alerts for this account to reduce noise.
Correct. AI flags patterns, but you own the relationship. Start with context (what actually happened?), then act. Escalation without context burns trust. Waiting burns time. The best first move is always: understand, then engage.
Slide 22 of 24 Application
Your first week: Set up alerts that actually help

The tool works best when you configure it for your specific workflow. Here is how to set up alerts that reduce noise and surface what matters.

  • Week 1: Start narrow. Set up one alert for one metric you already track manually (e.g., accounts with 3+ consecutive declining NPS scores). Test threshold settings.
  • Week 2: Add context triggers. Layer in alerts that combine metrics (declining sentiment + reduced meeting frequency). This catches patterns you would miss tracking one metric at a time.
  • Week 3: Integrate into workflow. Route alerts to your existing Slack channel (not a new one). Add insights to your weekly account review template.
  • Week 4: Refine thresholds. Review what triggered alerts. Adjust sensitivity to reduce false positives. Share your setup with your team.

Instructional Design Notes

ADDIE Framework Applied:

  • Analyze: Three distinct audiences (CS, Sales, Leadership) with different workflows and tool familiarity. No time for live training due to time zones.
  • Design: Scenario-based learning with embedded knowledge checks. Modular structure allows learners to skip to relevant sections.
  • Develop: 24 slides total. Synthesia avatar for consistency. Three quiz blocks placed after key concepts (slides 9, 15, 20). SCORM 1.2 for LMS compatibility.
  • Implement: Deployed via LMS with completion tracking. Integrated into new hire onboarding and quarterly tool refresh.
  • Evaluate: Measured adoption (tool usage data), not just completion. 67% active usage within 30 days vs. 18% industry benchmark for AI tool rollouts.

What makes this work: Real scenarios pulled from SME interviews. Quiz options designed to surface common misconceptions. Application slide gives concrete next steps, not just theory. Total module runtime: 18 minutes. Completion rate: 91%.
