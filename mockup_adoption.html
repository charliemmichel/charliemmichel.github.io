Program Architecture
Three tiers. Every employee. Role-appropriate depth.

Designed for a global workforce with wildly different starting points. Tier 1 is mandatory for everyone. Tiers 2 and 3 are role-triggered based on function and tool access. No one gets more than they need. No one gets less than the baseline.

01
Awareness
All employees · Mandatory
  • What the tool is and what it is not
  • Where it fits in daily workflow
  • Data handling, privacy, and acceptable use policy
  • Three scenario walkthroughs: before and after
  • When to use it vs. when not to
  • Where to get help and how to flag issues
Format: 24-slide SCORM module, avatar-narrated, 3 embedded quiz blocks. Avg completion: 18 min.
Pass threshold: 80% on all 3 quiz blocks.
02
Proficiency
Power users · Role-triggered
  • Prompt construction and iteration
  • Use case library: 12 function-specific scenarios
  • Output review and quality assessment
  • Integration with existing tools and workflows
  • Common failure modes and how to catch them
  • Sharing and standardizing outputs across the team
Format: 4-module async series + 1 live cohort session (recorded). Avg time: 3 hrs total.
Pass threshold: Practical exercise reviewed by manager.
03
Advanced
Champions · Nomination only
  • Building prompts for team-wide reuse
  • Evaluating new AI tools against org criteria
  • Facilitation: running an AI office hours session
  • Change management within your function
  • Flagging edge cases and escalation protocols
  • Feedback loop back to L&D and product teams
Format: Workshop series (3 sessions, 90 min each) + peer teaching capstone.
Outcome: Certified AI Champion, internal facilitator network.

LMS Administration
Every enrollment automated. Every exception documented.

Built to require zero manual enrollment from L&D after initial setup. HRIS sync handles new hire assignment. Role changes trigger re-enrollment in the appropriate tier. Completion data exports weekly to stakeholder dashboard.

Tier 1 — Enrollment and Completion Logic
TriggerHRIS new hire event OR manual L&D admin bulk upload for existing employees
Due dateNew hires: 14 days from start date. Existing employees at launch: 30 days from comms send.
Pass threshold80% on all 3 quiz blocks. Unlimited retakes with different question order each attempt.
Overdue logicDay 3 overdue → learner reminder. Day 10 → manager CC'd. Day 20 → L&D flagged for escalation.
Completion recordTimestamp, score, attempts, and pass date stored. Exports to HRIS compliance record quarterly.
SCORM standardSCORM 1.2. Tested across Chrome, Safari, and Edge. Mobile-compatible.
Tier 2 and 3 — Role-Triggered Logic
Tier 2 triggerJob function field in HRIS matches approved list (Sales, PMM, CS, People). Tier 1 completion required first.
Tier 3 triggerManual nomination by manager + L&D approval. Capacity capped at 20 per cohort.
Prerequisite gatesTier 2 locked until Tier 1 complete. Tier 3 locked until Tier 2 complete AND nomination approved.
Cohort groupingTier 2 auto-grouped by region for live session scheduling. Tier 3 cohorts formed quarterly.
Role change logicJob function change in HRIS triggers re-evaluation. New tier assigned automatically within 48 hrs.
Champion badgeTier 3 completions receive digital badge issued via LMS. Visible on internal profile and org directory.

Behavior Change Tracking
Completion is the start. Adoption is the goal.

Post-completion, adoption is tracked through a combination of tool usage data, manager observation check-ins, and a 30-day pulse survey. The goal is to know whether people are actually using what they learned, not just whether they finished the module.

Kirkpatrick Measurement — L1 through L4
Every level measured. Every result used.

The program is iterated quarterly based on what the data says. Underperforming quiz items get rewritten. Low-confidence survey results trigger office hours scheduling. Manager observation gaps trigger coaching for managers, not just for learners.

Level 1 — Reaction
Did it land?

5-question pulse survey sent immediately after Tier 1 completion. Covers content clarity, pacing, relevance to daily work, and avatar quality. Results reviewed by L&D within 48 hours of each completion wave.

Finding: 89% found content relevant to their role. Lowest-rated item was quiz pacing — addressed in v1.2 by adding scenario context before each quiz block.
Level 2 — Learning
Did they get it?

Three embedded quiz blocks at key retention moments. Question bank rotated quarterly. Wrong answers trigger remediation slide before re-attempt. Score and attempt count logged per user.

Finding: Average score 86%. Lowest-scoring item was data privacy scenario — rewrote with more concrete example in v1.1. Score jumped from 61% to 79% on that item.
Level 3 — Behavior
Did they change what they do?

30-day post-completion pulse (2 questions), manager observation form at 45 days, and office hours Q&A patterns analyzed for recurring friction points. Themes fed back into Tier 2 curriculum design.

Finding: 78% self-reported active use at day 30. Top barrier: "not sure when it's appropriate to use it" — added decision tree to Tier 1 in v1.2.
Level 4 — Results
Did the org move?

Tracked against two org-level goals: reduction in AI-related support tickets and increase in cross-functional AI tool proposals. Reviewed with senior leadership quarterly. Program recognized as org standard for all future tool launches.

Finding: 62% reduction in AI support tickets in first 60 days post-launch. Program adopted as organizational template — cited in CEO recognition of L&D team.
