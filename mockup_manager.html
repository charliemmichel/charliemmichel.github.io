6-Month Curriculum Architecture
Six months. Twelve sessions. One cohort that holds each other accountable.

Built on the 70-20-10 model. 10% formal learning, 20% peer cohort and coaching, 70% on-the-job application with structured reflection. Each month targets a different management competency with an applied practice component before the next session.

Month 1
Identity and Foundations
  • Manager vs. individual contributor mindset shift
  • Defining your management philosophy
  • Understanding your team's existing dynamics
  • Your first 30 days in the role — a playbook
  • 1:1 structure and cadence setup
Format: 90-min live kickoff session + async reflection module
Practice: Draft your management philosophy doc, share with cohort
Month 2
Feedback and Communication
  • The SBI feedback model in practice
  • Delivering difficult feedback without softening the message
  • Receiving upward feedback without defensiveness
  • Running effective 1:1s that surface real problems
  • Written vs. verbal communication decisions
Format: 90-min live session + 2 async skill modules
Practice: Deliver one structured feedback conversation, debrief at next session
Month 3
Performance Management
  • Setting goals that are measurable and motivating
  • Mid-cycle performance conversations
  • Managing underperformance before it becomes a PIP
  • Connecting individual goals to team and org priorities
  • Documentation: when and how
Format: 90-min live + HRIS performance tool walkthrough module
Practice: Complete goals framework for each direct report
Month 4
Hiring and Team Building
  • Writing job descriptions that attract the right people
  • Structured interview design and bias mitigation
  • Evaluation rubrics and debrief facilitation
  • Onboarding your own hire: setting them up to ramp
  • Building psychological safety in a new team
Format: 90-min live + async interview design module
Practice: Build an interview rubric for your next open role
Month 5
Navigating Up and Across
  • Managing your relationship with your manager
  • Cross-functional influence without authority
  • Presenting to leadership: what they actually want to hear
  • Escalation decisions: what to bring up and when
  • Protecting your team without burning bridges
Format: 90-min live panel with senior leaders + async module
Practice: Draft one upward communication — present to cohort for feedback
Month 6
Sustaining and Growing
  • Avoiding burnout: your capacity and your team's
  • Developing your direct reports toward their next role
  • Building your management style over time
  • Cohort capstone: what changed, what you'll do differently
  • Transition to manager peer community
Format: 90-min live capstone + celebration + peer community launch
Practice: 6-month retrospective submitted to L&D

LMS Administration
Cohort-based enrollment with automatic sequencing.

Each cohort is enrolled as a group with shared due dates. Async modules unlock monthly based on cohort start date. Live session attendance is manually logged by L&D admin after each session. Completion requires both async and live components.

Enrollment and Access Logic
Enrollment triggerManual — L&D admin enrolls cohort as a group 1 week before kickoff. Max 16 per cohort.
Eligibility ruleManager role in HRIS for less than 18 months OR newly promoted. HR partner nominates. L&D confirms.
Module unlock scheduleAsync modules unlock on the 1st of each program month. Cannot be accessed ahead of schedule.
Live session loggingL&D admin marks attendance manually after each session. Absence triggers follow-up email with recording link.
Completion definitionAll 6 async modules complete (80%+ quiz score) AND attendance at 5 of 6 live sessions.
Certificate issuedAuto-issued via LMS upon full completion. Digital + PDF version. Added to HRIS personnel record.

Measurement Framework
The program is judged by what changes in the team, not what the manager learned.

Primary KPI is direct report eNPS at 6 months post-program completion, compared to pre-program baseline. Secondary KPIs include manager self-efficacy score, 360 feedback delta, and time-to-performance-conversation for underperformers.

Level 1 — Reaction
Did it feel useful in the moment?

3-question pulse after each live session. Questions: relevance to current challenges, quality of peer discussion, one thing they'll apply this week. Results reviewed before next session to adjust facilitation.

Result: Avg 4.6/5 across all sessions. Month 3 (performance management) consistently rated highest relevance. Month 1 pacing flagged — reduced content density for next cohort.
Level 2 — Learning
Did they internalize the frameworks?

Async module quiz scores (80% threshold) and a mid-program 360 at Month 3. 360 asks direct reports to rate manager on 8 behaviors tied to program competencies. Baseline taken at Month 1.

Result: Average async quiz score 88%. 360 delta at Month 3: +0.6 on feedback quality (scale of 1–5), largest improvement area across all cohorts to date.
Level 3 — Behavior
Did they manage differently?

Post-program 360 at Month 7 (1 month after completion). HR partner observation notes from any PIPs or performance conversations initiated during program. Applied practice completion as leading indicator.

Result: 360 delta Month 1 to Month 7: +1.1 on "gives clear and actionable feedback" item. 34% increase in documented 1:1 notes logged in HRIS vs. pre-program cohort.
Level 4 — Results
Did their teams notice?

Direct report eNPS survey sent 6 months post-program completion. Compared to pre-program baseline for same team. Attrition rate for direct reports during and after program tracked against company average.

Result: 28% improvement in direct report eNPS at 6-month mark. Direct report attrition for program participants ran 14% below company average in the 12 months following completion.
