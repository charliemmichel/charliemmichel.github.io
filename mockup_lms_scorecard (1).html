Platform Evaluation Framework

A repeatable methodology for cross-functional technology decisions

Framework Overview

Use case: Evaluating enterprise platforms where stakeholders have competing priorities, budgets are high, and the wrong decision is expensive to reverse. Built for LMS selection but transferable to any major tech decision.

What this framework solves: Most platform evaluations fail because of politics, not features. This framework forces alignment on criteria before anyone sees a demo. It weights what matters. It surfaces disagreement early. It produces a defensible recommendation.

Real application: Used this to manage a cross-functional LMS evaluation across L&D, Revenue Enablement, Customer Success, and HR. 13 reviewers, 2 vendor POCs, 6-week timeline, $300K+ annual contract decision.

Step 1: Stakeholder Mapping

Identify Who Owns What

Executive Sponsor

Final decision authority. Signs contract. Needs business case, not feature list.

  • Budget approval
  • Risk tolerance
  • Strategic alignment

Primary Admins

Will configure and maintain platform daily. Cares most about usability.

  • Admin workflows
  • Content authoring
  • Support quality

End Users

Learners or platform consumers. If they hate it, adoption fails.

  • Learner experience
  • Mobile access
  • Speed and reliability

Cross-Functional Partners

Teams with adjacent needs. Integration points matter most.

  • Data access
  • Reporting needs
  • System integrations
Step 2: Build Weighted Criteria

Evaluation Criteria Template

Learning paths and compliance tracking 20% Primary use case. Must handle complex role-based assignments.
Reporting and analytics 15% Execs need completion data. Partners need custom exports.

Admin interface and workflows 15% Friction here compounds. Bad UX = admins resist using it.

Step 3: Run Structured POCs

Before the POC

Most POCs fail because vendors control the agenda. Fix this by defining success criteria first.

  • Build a test content library (real modules, real complexity)
  • Write scenario-based tasks for evaluators to complete
  • Set time limits (2 weeks max, forces focus)
  • Assign specific reviewers to specific criteria

During the POC

Evaluators should test, not watch demos. Hands-on experience surfaces issues faster than vendor presentations.

  • Everyone tests independently (no groupthink)
  • Document blockers in real-time
  • Track time spent on common tasks
  • Compare notes only after individual scoring

After the POC

Aggregate scores reveal patterns. Outliers reveal deal-breakers or personal preferences.

  • Calculate weighted composite scores per vendor
  • Surface disagreements (why did reviewers split?)
  • Identify must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
  • Flag contract or integration risks

Make the Recommendation

Present to exec sponsor with data, not opinions. Show the math. Acknowledge tradeoffs.

  • Lead with composite scores by category
  • Surface critical gaps or risks
  • Recommend a vendor with confidence level
  • Outline implementation risks and timeline

Why This Framework Works

Forces early alignment. Building weighted criteria before seeing vendors eliminates "I like this one because it looks nicer" decisions. You argue about priorities once, not continuously.

Surfaces hidden requirements. Cross-functional teams discover integration needs during criteria building that would have been deal-breakers post-contract.

Creates defensible decisions. When an exec questions the choice, you have weighted data. When a stakeholder complains later, you can show they agreed to the criteria upfront.

Scales beyond LMS. Used this exact framework for CRM, project management tools, and HR systems. The criteria change, the process stays the same.
