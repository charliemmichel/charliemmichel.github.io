Before You Start — Should I Use the Tool for This?
Does this task involve confidential data? Client contracts, personal data, financial records, anything marked "confidential"
Yes → No ↓
Check first. Contact Legal or IT before inputting. See acceptable use policy on Confluence.
Does this task require real-time or live data? Current prices, live system status, today's news, real-time metrics
Yes → No ↓
Do not use the tool. The tool does not browse the internet or access live data. Use the right source.
Does this require a final decision or professional judgment? Legal advice, medical guidance, HR decisions, client commitments
Yes → No ↓
Use with caution. Tool can help you draft or organize, but you own the final call. Do not cite the tool's output as authoritative.
Is this a drafting, summarizing, analyzing, or formatting task? Emails, summaries, reports, research synthesis, content outlines
Yes → —
You're clear to proceed. Build a specific prompt using the formula below. Always review output before using.
How to Build a Good Prompt — The Formula
1 — Role
Who you are
"As a Client Success Manager..."
2 — Task
What you need
"...draft a 2-page summary..."
3 — Context
What you're working with
"...of the following account notes from Q1..."
4 — Format
How you want it
"...in bullet points with an exec summary at the top..."
5 — Constraints
What to avoid
"...no jargon, keep it under 500 words."
Weak Prompt
Summarize this.
No role, no format, no constraints. Output will be generic and probably not usable without significant rewriting.
Strong Prompt
As a Client Success Manager preparing a QBR, summarize the following account notes from Q1 into a 2-page executive summary. Use bullet points for key wins and risks. Lead with business impact. Audience: VP level. No jargon. Under 500 words.
Role, task, context, format, constraints. Output will be close to usable — review and edit before sending.
Approved Use Cases by Function
Your Role Good Use Cases Use With Caution Do Not Use For
Sales / AM Call prep, email drafting, deal research synthesis, QBR summaries Pricing summaries (verify against CRM) Contract language, client commitments
Client Success Status reports, renewal prep, health score narratives, meeting notes Escalation summaries (always review carefully) SLA interpretations, personal client data
Product Marketing Content drafts, competitive analysis synthesis, messaging iteration Competitor claims (fact-check every output) Approved product statements, legal language
People / HR / L&D JD drafts, policy summarization, training content, comms drafting Performance feedback (human judgment required) Employee records, compensation data
Engineering / Product Documentation drafts, spec writing, code review notes, README Architecture decisions (verify with your team) Production code without review, security configs
Finance / Legal Document summarization (non-confidential only) Contract clause explanations (not legal advice) Confidential financials, legal filings, client data
Always Remember
Review before you send.
The tool generates fast. Fast is not the same as accurate. Every output needs your eyes before it goes anywhere.
Your name goes on it.
If you submit it, you own it. The tool is a drafting assistant. The judgment is yours.
When in doubt, ask.

Design Rationale — Why a Job Aid
70-20-10 Principle

The module handles the 10% — formal learning. This job aid supports the 70% — on-the-job application. Designed to be open in a browser tab, not remembered from training.

Format Decision

Single-page reference with decision tree, formula, and use-case table. Printable at A4 and letter. Digital version links directly from the Confluence AI hub page and the LMS completion screen.

Measurement

Confluence page views tracked monthly. A 2-question pulse sent at 60 days post-module: "Have you used the quick reference guide?" and "Did it help you make a decision?" Results reviewed quarterly.
