Instructional Design Artifact · Storyboard

AI Enablement Module
Slide-by-slide production blueprint.

Module: Tier 1 Awareness — All Employees
Format: Synthesia avatar-narrated, SCORM 1.2
Duration: ~18 minutes
Slides: 24 total, 3 quiz blocks
Audience: Global, 3 regions, async delivery
Tools Used Synthesia PowerPoint (production base) SCORM 1.2 MindTickle LMS Confluence (SME review) Slack (stakeholder coordination) Camtasia (screen captures) Google Forms (feedback)
Learning Objectives — Bloom's Taxonomy

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

Remember / Understand
Describe what the AI tool does, what it does not do, and where it fits in their daily workflow without referencing documentation.
Bloom's Level 1–2 · Assessed: Quiz Block 1
Apply / Analyze
Identify at least two appropriate use cases for the tool in their specific role and explain why each use case meets the acceptable use criteria.
Bloom's Level 3–4 · Assessed: Quiz Block 2
Evaluate
Distinguish between situations where using the tool is appropriate versus where human judgment or escalation is required, using the decision framework provided.
Bloom's Level 5 · Assessed: Quiz Block 3

Section 1 — IntroductionSlides 1–3
01
Title / HookAvatar: on-screen, center frame

"Every week, thousands of decisions get made across this organization. Some take hours. Some take minutes. What if more of them could take seconds — without sacrificing quality? That is what this module is about. Not AI in the abstract. This tool. Your workflow. Starting now."

Title card: Full-bleed dark background. Module title centered. Subtle animated gradient. No logo until slide 3.

Trigger word: "Starting now" — avatar hand gesture. Avatar placement: center. Duration target: 45s.

02
AgendaAvatar: lower-left corner

"Here is what we are covering today. What this tool is and — just as importantly — what it is not. How to use it in your role. The rules that protect you and the organization. And how to get help when you need it. This should take about 18 minutes. You can pause, rewind, and revisit any section."

Agenda list: Four items appear sequentially as narrated. Clean numbered list. Warm paper background. Each item has a small icon.

Items animate in on trigger words. No auto-advance — learner clicks through. Screenshot capture: not needed.

03
ContextAvatar: right side

"Before we go further — why now? The organization has rolled out this tool because we believe it can save time, reduce friction, and help everyone focus on higher-value work. But tools only deliver value when people know how to use them well. That is what this module is for. Not to tell you to use it — to make sure you can."

Left side: Three-column stat layout. "X hours saved per week per user," "Used across Y functions," "Launched to Z employees." Stats animate in.

Stats are placeholder — confirm real numbers with SME before production. Trigger: "That is what this module is for."

Section 2 — What It IsSlides 4–7
04
ExplanationAvatar: left side

"Let's start with what this tool actually is. At its core, it is a large language model — which means it processes text, understands context, and generates responses based on patterns in data it was trained on. Think of it less like a search engine and more like a very well-read assistant that can draft, summarize, and analyze — but one that needs your direction to do it well."

Right side: Simple diagram showing input → process → output. Labels: "Your prompt," "AI reasoning," "Generated response." Clean, no clip art.

Diagram builds in three steps with narration. Avoid jargon visually even if narration uses it. Duration: ~55s.

05
ScenarioAvatar: off-screen (VO only)

"Here is what that looks like in practice. Maya is a Client Success Manager preparing for a quarterly business review. She needs to summarize three months of account activity into a two-page document. Normally that takes two hours. Watch what happens when she uses the tool."

Screen recording: Simulated tool interface. User types prompt: "Summarize the following account notes into a 2-page QBR summary..." Output appears. Total clip: ~30s. Camtasia capture.

Screen capture needed. Record in Camtasia. Use placeholder account data — no real client info. Trim to 30s max. Export at 1080p.

06
What It Is NotAvatar: center

"Now — what it is not. It is not a search engine. It does not browse the internet or pull live data. It is not always right — it can generate plausible-sounding information that is incorrect. It is not a replacement for your judgment. And it is not the right tool for every task. Knowing those limits is as important as knowing the capabilities."

Four-panel layout: Each "not" appears as a card with a simple icon and one-line descriptor. Red X icon used sparingly — one per card, top corner.

Cards animate in sequentially with narration triggers. No auto-advance. Learner must read all four before Next is enabled — set in SCORM logic.

07
Quiz Block 1Avatar: off

"Let's check your understanding before we move on. Three quick questions. Take your time."

Knowledge check: 3 multiple choice questions. Q1: What is the tool best described as? Q2: Which of these is NOT a limitation of the tool? Q3: When Maya used the tool for the QBR, what was her primary benefit?

Embedded in SCORM. Pass threshold: 2/3 correct. Wrong answer → remediation screen with explanation. Correct → positive reinforcement + continue. No retake limit on this block.

Section 3 — Acceptable UseSlides 8–12
08
PolicyAvatar: left side

"Before you use any AI tool at work, you need to understand three things: what data you can put into it, what you cannot, and what happens to everything you share. Let's cover each one clearly."

Three-column layout: "What you CAN input," "What you CANNOT input," "What happens to your data." Color-coded: green, red, blue. Simple iconography.

SME-confirmed content. Do not publish until Legal and IT have reviewed this slide specifically. Flag for compliance review in Confluence before recording.

09
ScenarioAvatar: off-screen VO

"Here is a scenario. Marcus works in Finance. He wants to use the tool to help draft a summary of a confidential vendor contract. Is that an appropriate use? Take a moment to think about it before we continue."

Split screen: Left — Marcus at desk (illustration style, no stock photo). Right — text of the decision he is facing. Pause point: learner must click "I've thought about it" to continue.

Pause enforced in SCORM — Next button hidden for 8 seconds. This creates cognitive processing time before reveal on slide 10.

10
RevealAvatar: center

"The answer is no — not without checking first. Confidential vendor contracts contain proprietary third-party data. Before inputting any document marked confidential, Marcus should check with Legal or IT. The rule is simple: when in doubt, check before you paste."

Decision tree revealed: "Is it confidential?" → Yes → "Check with Legal/IT first." → No → "Is it personal data?" → Yes → "Do not input." → No → "You're clear to proceed." Simple flowchart, clean lines.

Decision tree is a static screenshot from Figma wireframe. Export at 2x resolution for clarity at any screen size.

11
Use CasesAvatar: lower-right

"Now let's look at where the tool works well for your specific role. We have mapped the most common use cases by function. Find yours and take note of the two or three that apply most directly to your day-to-day."

Interactive tab layout: Six function tabs — Sales, CS, PMM, Engineering, People/HR, Finance. Each tab reveals 3 use cases with one-line descriptions. Learner clicks their function. Screenshot capture of tool interface embedded in each use case where applicable.

Tab interaction built in Storyline 360. Each tab state designed separately. Screenshot captures for Sales (QBR), CS (status report), PMM (content drafting) confirmed with SMEs.

12
Quiz Block 2Avatar: off

"Section check. Three questions on acceptable use. These are the ones that matter most from a compliance standpoint."

3 scenario-based questions: Q1: Aisha wants to paste a client's personal email address into the tool. Should she? Q2: Which of these is an appropriate use for the Finance team? Q3: Before using the tool with confidential data, what should you do?

Pass threshold: 3/3 required on this block — compliance-critical content. Wrong answer triggers mandatory remediation before retry. Max 3 attempts before L&D notified via LMS flag.

Section 4 — Using It WellSlides 13–20
13
PromptingAvatar: left side

"The output is only as good as the input. A vague prompt gets a vague answer. A clear, specific prompt — with context, a defined format, and the right constraints — gets something you can actually use. Let's look at the difference."

Before/after split: Left — weak prompt and weak output (highlighted in red). Right — strong prompt and strong output (highlighted in green). Real examples, not generic.

Use real example prompts approved by SME. Do not use Lorem Ipsum. Weak prompt: "Summarize this." Strong prompt: "Summarize the following in 3 bullet points for a VP audience, focusing on business impact." Duration: ~50s.

14
PracticeAvatar: off-screen VO

"Your turn. Below is a task you might actually face in your role. Draft a prompt that would get you a useful result. There is no single right answer — but I will show you three strong examples after you try."

Reflection activity: Text box with scenario description. Learner types their prompt attempt (no submission — self-reflection only). After 30 seconds, "See strong examples" button appears. Three example prompts slide in.

Text box is decorative — no data captured. JavaScript timer reveals button at 30s. This is a practice moment, not an assessment. Note in design doc.

17
Avatar FeatureAvatar: full screen, direct address

"One more thing before the final section. Always review the output before you use it. The tool is fast — but fast is not the same as accurate. Read what it gives you. Edit where needed. Put your name on it only when you are confident it reflects your judgment, not just its generation."

Avatar only: Full-frame avatar direct address. No slide content. This is a trust-building moment — the "voice of reason" beat before the compliance section. Warm but serious tone.

Avatar slide — no screenshot capture needed. Use Synthesia direct-camera avatar angle. This is slide 17 per production guide — avatar appears on slides 1, 17, and 23 only.

22
Quiz Block 3Avatar: off

"Final check. Three questions. These test your ability to make the right call in a real situation — not just recall what we covered."

3 decision-scenario questions: Q1: You get a AI-generated output that sounds confident but includes a statistic you cannot verify. What do you do? Q2: A colleague asks you to share your prompt for a client email you drafted. Is that okay? Q3: Where do you go first if you have a question about appropriate use?

Pass threshold: 2/3. Bloom's Level 5 — evaluation. Wrong answer triggers specific remediation per question (not generic). Each distractor written to address a common misconception confirmed in SME interviews.

Avatar full-frame close: Warm, direct. No slide content. Transition to completion screen on final word.
