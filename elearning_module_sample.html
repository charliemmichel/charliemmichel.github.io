Core Concept

The ask nobody makes until it's too late.

"I've been here three weeks and I still don't know who to go to when I have a question that touches Sales AND Marketing. I just end up guessing."

— Common new hire feedback, 30-day survey
  • Most new hires wait to be told how the org works. High performers ask in week one.
  • The question "who should I loop in on this?" is never a sign of weakness. It's how work gets unblocked.
  • Every team has an unofficial point of contact who isn't on any org chart. Find them early.
💡
The 30-day rule: In your first 30 days, your job is to listen more than you speak and to map more than you build. Everything you create after that will be better for it.
Skill Building

Three habits that build trust faster than anything else.

What high-trust new hires do differently

  • They confirm, don't assume. Before starting a cross-functional project, they send a two-line message that says: "Here's what I think we're doing and by when. Does that match your understanding?" Takes 90 seconds. Prevents weeks of rework.
  • They share progress before they're asked. A quick weekly note to your manager and key partners ("here's where things stand, here's where I need input") signals ownership without requiring a meeting.
  • They make it easy to help them. When they need something, they come with context. Not "can you help with this" — but "here's the situation, here's what I need from you, here's my deadline."
