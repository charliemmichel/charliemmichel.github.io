Onboarding Essentials: Working Across Teams 1 of 5
Module Introduction
Working across teams starts before day one.

This module covers the three things every new hire needs to understand about cross-functional collaboration in the first 30 days.

🗺️
Navigate the org
Know who owns what and why.
💬
Communicate early
Set expectations before they become assumptions.
✅
Build trust fast
Small actions in week one compound over months.
Core Concept
The ask nobody makes until it's too late.
"I've been here three weeks and I still don't know who to go to when a question touches Sales AND Marketing. I just end up guessing." — 30-day new hire survey
  • Most new hires wait to be told how the org works. High performers ask in week one.
  • The question "who should I loop in?" is never a sign of weakness. It's how work gets unblocked.
  • Every team has an unofficial point of contact who isn't on any org chart. Find them early.
The 30-day rule: In your first 30 days, your job is to listen more than you speak and to map more than you build.
Skill Building
Three habits that build trust faster than anything else.
  • They confirm, don't assume. Before starting a cross-functional project, they send a two-line message: "Here's what I think we're doing and by when. Does that match?" Takes 90 seconds. Prevents weeks of rework.
  • They share progress before being asked. A quick weekly note to key partners signals ownership without requiring a meeting.
  • They make it easy to help them. Not "can you help?" — but "here's the situation, here's what I need, here's my deadline."
Knowledge Check
