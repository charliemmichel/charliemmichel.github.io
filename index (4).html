Case Study
AI Enablement

How I built an AI tool rollout program that actually changed how people worked.

Three regions, different workflows, different levels of tool familiarity, and no shared time zone for live training. The program needed to work asynchronously and actually change behavior, not just produce a completion number.

Built the whole thing solo. Script, Synthesia avatar configuration, slide-by-slide trigger word mapping, screenshot captures, three embedded quiz blocks, SCORM packaging, and LMS deployment. The production pipeline became the repeatable template for every AI tool launch that followed.

The result: VP-level recognition on launch and adopted as the organizational standard for AI enablement. The content was substantive enough that the CEO watched the full module and followed up with questions, which mattered as confirmation that something had actually landed.

The ADDIE model and what most programs skip.

The most skipped phase: Evaluate. Most programs measure completion and call it done. But Kirkpatrick Level 3, behavior change on the job, is where real ROI lives. If behavior has not changed, the program did not work.
Application
What separates adoption from awareness.
"We rolled out the tool to everyone. Sent the training link. Completion rate was 94%. But three months later, barely anyone was actually using it."L&D Manager, post-launch retrospective
  • Awareness is not adoption. A high completion rate does not mean behavior has changed. It means people clicked through.
  • Adoption requires opportunity, motivation, and ability. Remove any one and the behavior will not stick, regardless of how good the training is.
  • The environment enables or prevents use. If the tool is not integrated into the workflow, the best training in the world will not move the needle.
Knowledge Check
A new AI summarization tool was rolled out company-wide. Training completion hit 91% in two weeks. But 60 days later, usage data shows only 18% of employees are using it regularly. What does this most likely indicate?
✓  Correct. High completion confirms exposure, not behavior change. Sustainable adoption requires workflow integration, manager reinforcement, and ongoing performance support beyond the initial training event.
Not quite. The completion data shows people engaged with the training. The gap between 91% completion and 18% usage is the classic awareness-vs-adoption problem. Training alone rarely changes behavior without workflow integration alongside it.
