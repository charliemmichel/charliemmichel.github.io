Core Concept
The ask nobody makes until it's too late.
"I've been here three weeks and I still don't know who to go to when a question touches both Sales and Marketing. I just end up guessing." — 30-day new hire survey
  • Most new hires wait to be told how the org works. High performers ask in week one.
  • The question "who should I loop in?" is never weakness — it's how work gets unblocked.
  • Every team has an unofficial go-to person who isn't on any org chart. Find them early.
The 30-day rule: In your first 30 days, listen more than you speak and map more than you build. Everything you create after that will be better for it.
Skill Building
Three habits that build trust faster than anything else.
  • They confirm, don't assume. Before starting a cross-functional project, a two-line message: "Here's what I think we're doing. Does that match?" 90 seconds. Prevents weeks of rework.
  • They share progress before being asked. A quick weekly note to key partners signals ownership without requiring a meeting.
  • They make it easy to help them. Not "can you help?" — but "here's the situation, here's what I need, here's my deadline."
