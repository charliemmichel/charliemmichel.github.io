About

I am a Senior Program Manager on the L&D team at a global ad tech company, where I own onboarding and enablement for 1,000+ employees across the US, India, and EMEA.

I have worked across the full operating cycle of a tech company. From the HRIS trigger that starts an offer to how a new hire shows up in Salesforce six months later. I have partnered with recruiting, CS, HR, and GTM teams, sat inside client support operations to understand the work firsthand, and built the tracking infrastructure to measure whether programs actually moved anything.

I build AI enablement programs, onboarding systems, cross-functional training, manager development, mentorship structures, and internal communications. I consult on capability gaps at the executive level and also fix the broken Qualtrics form. I do not think of those as different categories of work.

What drives me is making organizations run cleanly. Nothing falling through the cracks, every handoff accounted for, and people genuinely equipped to do their jobs well. I want to do that work somewhere the mission matters to me too.

Case Study
Value Creator Award 2026

How I built an AI enablement program that earned a CEO-signed award.

Three regions, different workflows, different levels of tool familiarity, and no shared time zone for live training. The program needed to work asynchronously and actually change behavior, not just produce a completion number.

Built the whole thing solo in under one month. Script, Synthesia avatar configuration, slide-by-slide trigger word mapping, screenshot captures, three embedded quiz blocks, SCORM packaging, and LMS deployment. The production pipeline became the repeatable template for every AI tool launch that followed.

The result: CEO-recognized for "building the adoption engine, not just executing training." VP-level recognition on launch and adopted as the organizational standard for AI enablement. The content was substantive enough that the CEO watched the full module and followed up with questions.

Week 1
Audience analysis and gap assessment
Three distinct learner groups across US, India, and EMEA with different tool familiarity, workflow contexts, and time zone constraints. No budget for live facilitation.
Week 2
Instructional design and content development
24-slide scenario-based module with three embedded quiz blocks at key retention moments. Synthesia avatar configured, trigger words mapped, screenshot captures staged.
Week 3
Production and SCORM packaging
Full production pipeline built solo: video generation, slide assembly, SCORM 1.2 packaging, LMS testing across three regions.
Week 4
Global deployment and executive impact
Launched to 1,000+ employees. VP recognition on launch day. CEO watched in full and sent follow-up questions. Nominated for Value Creator Award by L&D Director and Executive Sponsor.
Outcome
A solo-built module delivered in under one month that reached 1,000+ employees across 3 regions, earned a CEO-signed award, and became the organizational template for every AI tool rollout that followed.
