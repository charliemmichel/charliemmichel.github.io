About

I build the thing.
Then I make it stick.

Most enablement work stops at content delivery. I design for behavior change, which means thinking about the manager who forgets to reinforce the training, the new hire in a different time zone who never gets a live session, and the VP who wants to know if it actually worked.

My background spans the full operating cycle: HRIS triggers, day-one experience, 30-60-90 structure, sales methodology enablement, manager development, and ongoing performance support. I have built inside ad tech, freight logistics, automotive retail, and media.

Before my current role: field coach for a national EV launch, traveling the Southeast and designing on the ground. Before that: embedded in BPO operations at a freight startup, building training to match how work actually happened. Every role taught me to get close to the work before designing for it.

That is the thing that makes the programs land.

Case Study
Value Creator Award 2026

How I built an AI program that the CEO recognized without being asked to.

Three regions. No live facilitation budget. No shared time zone. The program needed to actually change behavior, not log a completion.

Built entirely solo in under one month. Script, avatar configuration, production mapping, three embedded quiz blocks, SCORM 1.2 packaging, LMS deployment, and a production playbook so every AI tool launch that followed could use the same template.

The result: Recognized at the executive level for building the adoption engine, not just executing training. Became the organizational standard. Senior leadership completed the full module and followed up with questions.

Week by week
Week 1
Audience analysis and gap assessment
Three learner groups. Different familiarity, different workflows, no budget for facilitation. Mapped every gap before touching a slide.
Week 2
Instructional design and content build
24 scenario-based slides. Quiz blocks at key retention moments. Avatar configured, trigger words mapped, screenshot captures staged and sequenced.
Week 3
Production and SCORM packaging
Video generation, slide assembly, SCORM 1.2 packaging, LMS testing across three regions. Solo. Production guide written in parallel for reuse.
Week 4
Global launch and executive impact
1,000+ employees reached day one. VP recognition at launch. Senior leadership engaged the full module and followed up with questions. Nominated for the Value Creator Award.

Under 30 days. Solo. 1,000+ employees across three regions. CEO-signed award. Every AI rollout that followed used the same playbook.
